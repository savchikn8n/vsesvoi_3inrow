# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Players open the Telegram Mini App from the Vsesvoi hookah lounge community, usually on a phone, between social moments, bookings, and visits. Staff and the owner use the dashboard to keep operations safe: promos, gifts, broadcasts, feedback, analytics, and maintenance mode.

## Product Purpose

The product turns attention around the venue into a fast, polished match-3 mini-game where players earn claps, compete for records, and redeem venue rewards. Success means the game feels like a real lightweight mobile game, keeps every player's progress intact, and gives staff clear operational controls.

## Brand Personality

Bold, playful, premium. The experience should feel black-and-gold, energetic, and venue-native: not casino clutter, not generic SaaS, and not a rough prototype.

## Anti-references

Avoid anything that feels "vibe-coded": janky animations, inconsistent controls, cheap effects, overflowing text, unclear tap targets, or UI that looks decorative before it is useful. Avoid resetting, hiding, or confusing player progress. Avoid slow motion that makes the game feel less responsive.

## Design Principles

1. Protect player trust before visual ambition.
2. Make the game feel fast first, then beautiful.
3. Use motion to explain state: swaps, matches, cascades, rewards, and blocked actions.
4. Keep the black-and-gold identity strong without drowning every surface in decoration.
5. Dashboard changes must stay operational, scannable, and reversible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The app should respect reduced-motion preferences, keep text readable on small Telegram viewports, preserve clear focus/active states, and avoid color-only feedback for important states. Animations should degrade to short fades or instant state changes when motion is reduced.
